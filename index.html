Friends of Cabrillo Bridge

Cayucos, CA • Community-led effort to restore ADA-friendly access across Cabrillo Bridge

Our Purpose

To reopen Cabrillo Bridge for motorized mobility devices (ADA accessible), Low Speed Vehicles (LSV), and golf carts. The bridge had been open to these devices for over 15 years without incident or complaint. On March 10, 2025 the County abruptly closed the bridge due to a citizen complaint.

Why This Matters

San Luis Obispo County controls the bridge. In pursuing the reopening of Cabrillo Bridge, we have met with County Supervisor Bruce Gibson, Public Works Director John Diodatti, and county staff to discuss options to reopen the bridge, create another route, or develop a surface road to connect south and north Cayucos.

Cayucos is split in half. There is currently no way to drive a motorized mobility device or cart between the two sections of Cayucos. Residents in south Cayucos who depend on these devices cannot reach essential services downtown—market, drug store, post office, eateries—or visit family and loved ones. Mobility devices and carts are not allowed on HWY 1, and many elderly residents do not want to drive on a 65 MPH highway.

Public support is strong. We have collected over 750 signatures of residents, business owners, and visitors to reopen the Cabrillo Bridge.
